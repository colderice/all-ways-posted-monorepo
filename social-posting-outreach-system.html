The Core Positioning Rule

"We handle the posting. What happens after is on you."

You are selling a done-for-you content posting service — not a marketing strategy, not a lead generation system, not a growth guarantee. The product is consistency and relief from the frustration of never posting. That's it. That's the whole thing.

3-Email Outreach Sequence

Personalization Brief (Realtors / Brokerages)
Trigger: Their social feed is inactive, inconsistent, or clearly generic  ·  Pain: They know they should post but never do  ·  Bridge: You already made their content — they see it before they commit
Email 1 · Day 1
Subject: made something for {{companyName}}
~75 words
Hi {{firstName}}, Most realtors and brokerage owners I talk to have the same Saturday morning moment — they sit down to write posts, stare at a blank screen for 20 minutes, then just close the tab. I already created two weeks of content for you, fully matched to {{companyName}}'s brand. I want to send it over to you — no charge whatsoever, no strings, no sales pitch. Just a free 2-week sample so you can see what it's like when your feed is actually consistent. Want me to send it over? — John
WHY IT WORKS: The "Saturday morning" scene hits before the pitch — prospect recognizes themselves in it. No promise of results. No mention of growth or leads. The ask is a yes/no — lowest friction possible.
Email 2 · Day 3
Subject: re: made something for {{companyName}}
~90 words
Hi {{firstName}}, Still have those 2 weeks of content ready for you. I have a client who told me she used to spend every Sunday night stressing about what to post that week. First Monday after she signed up, she spent that time on an actual client call instead. That's the whole point. I handle the posting. 20 on-brand posts a month, delivered every Friday. You don't have to think about it, write it, or design it. I'm not promising you new clients — I'm promising you a consistent feed. $497/month. Want the free content? — John
WHY IT WORKS: The client story is one sentence and immediately concrete — Sunday stress vs. a real client call. Then the positioning line lands harder: "I'm not promising you new clients — I'm promising you a consistent feed." Disarms skepticism, sets honest expectations.
Email 3 · Day 7
Subject: closing the loop
~65 words
Hi {{firstName}}, Last note. Somewhere between the client calls, the invoices, and everything else running through your head this week — posting to social probably didn't make the list. It rarely does. If you ever get tired of your feed sitting empty while the rest of the business moves, I'm one reply away. The free content I built for you is still here. — John
WHY IT WORKS: Opens with a true-to-life scene — no accusations, just recognition. "It rarely does" is empathy, not pressure. Closes the door gently with the free content as a soft re-entry point.

Response Scripts

They're Interested — "Send it over" / "Tell me more"
Positive
Hi {{firstName}}, Sending the content now — take a look and see how it feels. Here's exactly what you get for $497/month: · 20 on-brand posts per month · 5 posts delivered every Friday · Captions and visuals matched to your brand · No contract — cancel anytime with a week's notice I handle the posting side. What you do with it on your end is up to you. Ready to get started? I'll send the invoice today. — John
Objections
Handle
"Will this actually bring in new customers?"
Honest answer — that's not what I sell. I deliver consistent, on-brand posts to your feed every week. Whether that turns into new customers depends on your offer, your market, and how you engage with it. What I can promise is your feed won't be sitting empty anymore. That part I control completely.
"We already post sometimes"
Totally fair. Most businesses do — just not consistently. The frustration isn't usually starting, it's keeping up with it when you're busy. That's the problem I solve. You keep doing what you're doing, I make sure the posting never falls off.
"$497 feels like a lot"
It's about $25 a post. Most businesses spend more than that trying to throw something together last minute — or they just don't post at all. I'm not promising it pays for itself in new business. I'm saying your feed stays active and on-brand every single week without you touching it. That's the value.
"What if I don't like the content?"
You see everything before it goes anywhere. You can give feedback, request changes, or skip a week. I'm handling the execution — you stay in control of what actually goes out.
Not Interested
Rejection
No worries at all — appreciate you letting me know. If the posting ever becomes one of those things that keeps falling to the bottom of the list, you know where to find me. — John
No Response After All 3 Emails
Silence
Archive. Do not send a 4th email. Mark as "sequence complete — no reply" in your tracker. Revisit in 60–90 days only if you see a new trigger: · They start posting again then go quiet · New location or service announced · A seasonal moment relevant to their business
