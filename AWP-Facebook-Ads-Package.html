Creative Direction

The audit tool already has strong visual branding (navy/gold, letter grades, clean report layout). Lean into that in the creative. The "report card" format is instantly recognizable and creates curiosity. Three creative formats below — start with the static image, add video when you can.

Creative Option 1 — Static Image (Start Here)

Recommended First

Concept: A mockup of the audit report — specifically the scorecard section showing letter grades (A, C+, B, F) across the 5 categories. The image makes the product tangible before the click.

Background: Navy dark (#121B27) with a subtle gold radial glow.

Main visual: The score breakdown card from the report — big letter grade center left (e.g. "C+" in amber), five category bars with grades. Looks like an actual report someone received.

Text overlay (top): "YOUR BUSINESS JUST GOT GRADED" in Barlow Condensed white, all caps, 40px+.

Text overlay (bottom): "Find out your score — free, instant, no login." in smaller white text.

AWP logo: Bottom right corner, small.

Size: 1080x1080 (square, works across feed and stories). Also export 1200x628 for link preview.

Tool to build it: Use Genspark or Canva. Export the actual report HTML as a screenshot and mock it inside the creative frame — more authentic than illustration.

Creative Option 2 — Video (15–30 sec)

Add Week 2

Hook (0–3 sec): Text on screen, large: "Does your business pass or fail this test?" — no voiceover needed, captions carry it.

Middle (3–18 sec): Screen recording of the audit intake form being filled out for a real local business (use a demo URL). Show the 3-step form moving quickly, then the loading animation, then the report appearing on screen with the letter grade revealed.

End frame (18–28 sec): Static frame on navy background — "Free Brand Presence Audit" + "allwaysposted.com" + AWP logo. Text: "Takes 2 minutes. 100% free."

Captions: Auto-generate in Meta's ad creator. 85% of video ads are watched on mute.

Aspect ratio: 9:16 for Reels and Stories; 1:1 for feed. Record in 9:16, crop to square.

Tone: Clean, no music needed — the report reveal is the hook. Keep it under 30 seconds.

Creative Option 3 — Carousel (Pain Points)

Test Week 3+

Concept: Each card = one common brand presence failure. Builds urgency by showing the problems one at a time before the CTA card.

Card 1: Navy bg + gold text — "Your Google search snippet is being written by an algorithm right now." Sub: "No meta description = no control."

Card 2: "Your website has no sitemap. Google is finding your pages on its own." Sub: "That means some pages aren't indexed at all."

Card 3: "Your social profiles aren't linked from your own website." Sub: "You're making customers go find you."

Card 4: "Your Open Graph title reads 'Home.'" Sub: "Every time your URL gets shared, that's what people see."

Card 5 (CTA): Gold bg + navy text — "Find out what's broken. Free brand audit. Takes 2 minutes." + AWP logo.

Design note: Keep each card minimal — one bold statement, one supporting line. All uppercase headlines using Barlow Condensed or similar compressed font.

Landing Page Review

Overall Assessment

The intake form is clean, well-branded, and low-friction. Three-step flow is smart — it progressively reveals fields instead of dumping everything on one screen. The design matches the report, which builds trust. The loading animation with progress steps is a nice touch. There are five specific things to fix before running paid traffic.

⚠️
No trust signal at the email field
Step 3 asks for email but has no anti-spam language. Cold traffic is skeptical. When you're sending someone from a Facebook ad to an unknown brand and asking for their email, you need to kill the hesitation right there on the form.
Fix: Add "No spam. Your report arrives on screen instantly." as a sub-label or hint under the email input field.
🔴
Success state copy is wrong for the actual UX
The success state says "Your Report Is On Its Way — Check your inbox in the next few minutes." But the code actually redirects to the report URL on screen within 800ms of completion. The email is the backup, not the primary delivery. This creates confusion — user thinks they need to go check email but the report is already showing.
Fix: Change success copy to "Your report is loading now — redirecting you in a moment." Or just let the redirect happen and remove the success state entirely.
⚡
Hero copy doesn't say "instant on-screen" clearly enough
The hero currently says "generate a full live report." The word "live" does some work but "instant, on your screen" is the hook that separates this from every other form that says "we'll email you something." That instant gratification is the conversion driver and it should be louder.
Fix: Update hero body to: "We'll analyze your website and social media presence, then show you a full report — live on your screen in about 90 seconds. No email required. No waiting."
💡
Step 2 social handles have no placeholder examples
The social handle inputs have no placeholder text. Some small business owners won't know what a "handle" is or what format to use. A simple placeholder like "@yourbusiness" or "facebook.com/yourbusiness" removes that micro-friction.
Fix: Add placeholder="e.g. @yourbusiness" to each social input field.
🏆
No social proof anywhere on the page
Cold traffic from Facebook ads hits a page from a brand they've never heard of and is asked to enter their business URL and email. There's nothing on the page that says anyone else has used this, trusts it, or found it useful. Even one testimonial or a "Used by 200+ businesses" statement would help.
Fix: After you've run 10–20 audits, add a single testimonial quote just above the form, or a "Trusted by 500 small businesses" line in the hero.
What's Working — Don't Touch
  • Three-step flow keeps it from feeling like a long form
  • "What You're Getting" box on Step 3 is strong — it handles objections right before submission
  • The gold submit button with "Run My Free Audit 🚀" is a solid CTA
  • Loading animation with the progress checklist keeps users from bouncing during the crawl
  • The "Free · No Paid Tools · Generated Instantly" tag at the top of the hero is excellent
  • Mobile responsive layout looks clean

Launch Sequence

Before You Launch
  • Deploy the audit app and confirm it works end-to-end
  • Apply the 5 landing page fixes above
  • Set up the Google Sheets webhook for lead capture
  • Configure the email sender (SendGrid recommended)
  • Test the full form flow from a mobile device
  • Set up Facebook Pixel on the audit page
  • Create a "Thank You" conversion event on report view
Days 1–7 (Learning Phase)
  • Launch all 3 ad variations simultaneously
  • Set budget at $15/day, don't touch it
  • Do not edit the ad set — it resets the learning phase
  • Monitor CTR and cost per click daily
  • Track how many people complete the form (not just click)
  • Expect CPCs of $0.80–$2.50 for this audience
Days 8–30 (Optimize)
  • Kill the two lowest-CTR ad variations
  • Double budget on the winner: $30–$40/day
  • Test a second ad set with Advantage+ audience
  • Build a retargeting campaign for page visitors who didn't submit
  • If video is ready, launch the Reels ad
  • Add a lookalike audience based on form completers
Key metric to watch: Form completion rate (people who submit vs. people who click the ad). If it drops below 20%, the landing page has a friction problem. If CTR on the ad is below 0.8%, the creative or copy needs to change — not the targeting.
